### Definition Tab Content
```html
<h2>What is Glaucoma?</h2>
<p>Glaucoma is a group of eye conditions characterized by damage to the optic nerve, usually 
caused by abnormally high intraocular pressure. The optic nerve is responsible for transmitting 
visual information from the eye to the brain.</p>

<h3 style="margin-top: 20px;">Types of Glaucoma:</h3>
<ul style="margin-left: 20px; margin-top: 10px;">
    <li><strong>Open-angle glaucoma:</strong> The most common type, developing gradually over time.</li>
    <li><strong>Angle-closure glaucoma:</strong> A less common but more acute form requiring immediate treatment.</li>
    <li><strong>Normal-tension glaucoma:</strong> Occurs with normal eye pressure but still causes optic nerve damage.</li>
    <li><strong>Secondary glaucoma:</strong> Results from other eye conditions or diseases.</li>
</ul>
```

### Management & Treatment Tab
```html
<h2>Management & Treatment Options</h2>

<h3 style="margin-top: 20px;">Treatment Goals:</h3>
<p>The primary goal is to lower intraocular pressure to prevent further optic nerve damage.</p>

<h3 style="margin-top: 20px;">1. Medications (First-line treatment)</h3>
<ul style="margin-left: 20px; margin-top: 10px;">
    <li><strong>Prostaglandin analogs:</strong> Increase fluid outflow from the eye</li>
    <li><strong>Beta-blockers:</strong> Reduce fluid production</li>
    <li><strong>Carbonic anhydrase inhibitors:</strong> Decrease fluid production</li>
</ul>

<h3 style="margin-top: 20px;">2. Surgical Options</h3>
<ul style="margin-left: 20px; margin-top: 10px;">
    <li><strong>Laser surgery (trabeculoplasty):</strong> Opens drainage angles</li>
    <li><strong>Filtering surgery (trabeculectomy):</strong> Creates new drainage pathway</li>
    <li><strong>Minimally invasive glaucoma surgery (MIGS):</strong> Newer procedures with faster recovery</li>
</ul>

<h3 style="margin-top: 20px;">3. Lifestyle & Home Management</h3>
<ul style="margin-left: 20px; margin-top: 10px;">
    <li>Take medications consistently and on schedule</li>
    <li>Regular eye pressure monitoring</li>
    <li>Avoid strenuous activities that raise IOP</li>
    <li>Manage stress and maintain overall health</li>
</ul>
```


### Definition Tab
```html
<h2>What are Cataracts?</h2>
<p>A cataract is a clouding of the eye's natural lens, which lies behind the iris and pupil. 
The lens helps focus light onto the retina at the back of the eye. When the lens becomes cloudy, 
light cannot pass through clearly, resulting in blurry or dim vision.</p>

<h3 style="margin-top: 20px;">Types:</h3>
<ul style="margin-left: 20px; margin-top: 10px;">
    <li><strong>Age-related:</strong> Most common, develops with age</li>
    <li><strong>Congenital:</strong> Present at birth or early childhood</li>
    <li><strong>Secondary:</strong> Result from other eye diseases</li>
    <li><strong>Traumatic:</strong> Develop after eye injury</li>
</ul>
```

## Content Length Guidelines

- **Descriptions**: 1-2 sentences (50-150 words)
- **Definitions**: 2-3 paragraphs (150-300 words)
- **Symptoms**: 5-8 items per category
- **Risk Factors**: 3-5 items per category
- **Treatments**: 3-4 main options with details
- **Actions**: 5-8 specific actionable items

---

## Pro Tips for Content

1. **Use Simple Language**: Avoid medical jargon or explain it
2. **Be Specific**: Give concrete examples
3. **Organize Hierarchically**: H2 > H3 > bullets
4. **Add Context**: "Why this matters to you"
5. **Link Ideas**: Show connections between sections
6. **Emphasize Action**: Clear next steps
7. **Include Warnings**: Safety information first
8. **Be Evidence-Based**: Use reliable medical sources
